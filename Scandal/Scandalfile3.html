Shocking Corpse Trafficking Scandal Unfolds in Northern China

In a shocking turn of events, a scandal has erupted in northern China involving the illegal trafficking of
human corpses. Prosecutors have charged dozens of individuals in a scheme that lasted over a decade, where
bodies meant for cremation were sold to a state-affiliated biomaterial company called Shanxi Osteorad
Biomaterial Co.

The Scheme Unveiled

  • The investigation revealed that Shanxi Osteorad made nearly $53 million in revenue from 2015 to 2023 by
    illegally acquiring over 4,000 cadavers. This operation stretched across at least seven provinces, where
    bodies were not only transferred but also dissected, stored, and turned into materials used for
    bone-grafting procedures.

Who Was Involved?

  • At least 75 suspects have been identified in this scandal. These include funeral home managers, doctors,
    and shareholders of the involved companies. The investigation is still ongoing, with prosecutors working
    to uncover more details.

How It Worked

The scheme primarily involved funeral homes in southwestern provinces like Yunnan, Guizhou, and Sichuan.
These homes collected cremation fees from families but instead of cremating the bodies, they sold them to
hospitals for dissection and storage.

  • Some families paid for cremation services but chose not to take home the ashes, while others had
    unclaimed cadavers sold instead.

Conclusion

This scandal highlights serious ethical issues within the biomedical and funeral industries in China. It
raises important questions about oversight and the treatment of the deceased. As the investigation
continues, more troubling details may emerge, shedding light on this disturbing operation.
