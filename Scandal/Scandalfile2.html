Former Fundraiser Jack Olson Pleads Guilty in Omaha Corruption Scandal

In a significant development in a public corruption scandal in Omaha, Nebraska, Jack Olson, the former chief
fundraiser for the Omaha Latino Police Officers Association (LPOA), has officially entered a guilty plea.
This plea comes after a lengthy battle against the charges he faced alongside former police officers and a
former city councilman.

Charges and Plea

  • Charges: Olson pled guilty to two serious charges:
  • Tax Evasion: Failing to file tax returns for four consecutive years.
  • Conspiracy to Commit Wire Fraud: Involvement in a scheme to defraud donors.
  • Sentencing Date: He is scheduled to be sentenced on November 5 in Lincoln, Nebraska.

Background of the Case

  • Co-defendants: Olson was arrested alongside former City Councilman Vinny Palermo and police officers
    Richie Gonzalez and Johnny Palermo, all of whom also pled guilty to conspiracy charges.
  • Potential Penalties: The conspiracy charge carries a maximum prison sentence of 20 years, while the
    tax evasion charge could lead to five years in prison. However, as part of Olson's plea deal, his prison
    sentence will not exceed 42 months.

Details of the Fraud

  • Olson was accused of pocketing a significant portion of the funds he raised for the LPOA, keeping between 65% to 80% of all donations.
  • He misled donors by claiming that 100% of the funds would support a police athletic league aimed at helping underprivileged youth.
  • Total Amount Raised: Over a four-year period, Olson reportedly collected more than half a million dollars.

Tax Evasion Findings

  • Tax Loss: The federal indictment revealed that Olson failed to file income tax returns for the
    years 2018 through 2021, resulting in a federal tax loss of $95,705.

Restitution Ordered

    As part of the plea agreement, Olson has been ordered to pay restitution to various parties:

  • $27,111 to the Latino Peace Officers Association.
  • Up to $30,883 to individual donors, with the exact amount to be determined at sentencing.
  • $95,475 to the U.S. Department of the Treasury.

Conclusion

Jack Olson's guilty plea marks a significant turn in the ongoing investigation into public corruption in
Omaha. With sentencing approaching, the case highlights serious issues of financial misconduct and the
importance of accountability in fundraising efforts for community organizations.
