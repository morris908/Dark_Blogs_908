Large-Scale Extortion Campaign: How Hackers Exploited Exposed Files to Steal Data

Imagine leaving a key to your house under the doormat, thinking no one will find it. But what if someone did and used it to take your valuables? Something similar happened in a recent large-scale extortion campaign where hackers found and exploited exposed keys—or credentials—left out in the open by organizations.

What Happened?

  • Hackers targeted a type of file called a .env file, which is often used in web applications to store important information like passwords and access keys for cloud services and social media accounts. These files should be kept private, but in this case, they were accidentally left exposed on the internet, allowing anyone to access them.

  • Once the hackers found these exposed .env files, they used the information inside to break into the organizations' cloud services, like Amazon Web Services (AWS). From there, they launched further attacks, scanning millions of other websites and collecting even more sensitive information.

The Scale of the Attack

The hackers were highly organized and targeted over 110,000 domains, successfully stealing more than 90,000 pieces of sensitive data. This included cloud service credentials and social media account details. Instead of using traditional methods like exploiting security bugs, the attackers simply took advantage of the exposed files to get in.

What Did the Hackers Do?

  • Stole Data: They copied sensitive information from cloud storage and left ransom notes, demanding payment to prevent them from selling the data on the dark web.

  • Sent Phishing Emails: They used stolen Mailgun credentials to send phishing emails from legitimate domains, making it harder for victims to detect the scam.

  • Attempted Cryptocurrency Mining: They also tried to use the compromised cloud services to mine cryptocurrency, but this attempt failed.

Why Does This Matter?

  • This attack highlights the dangers of accidentally exposing sensitive information online. The hackers didn't need to find complex security flaws; they simply exploited simple mistakes like leaving important files unprotected. It also shows how skilled and knowledgeable these attackers were, using advanced techniques to automate their attacks and spread quickly across the internet.

Conclusion

Hackers found and exploited exposed .env files containing important credentials, allowing them to break into cloud services, steal data, and demand ransom. This large-scale attack affected thousands of organizations, showing the importance of securing sensitive information and being aware of potential vulnerabilities.
