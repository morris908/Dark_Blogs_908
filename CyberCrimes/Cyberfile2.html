LastPass Security Incident: What Happened and What You Need to Know

Imagine someone sneaking into a secure building, but instead of stealing anything valuable, they just take a look at some blueprints. That's similar to what happened recently with LastPass, a popular password manager used by millions to keep their online accounts safe.

On August 25th, LastPass announced that an unauthorized person managed to get into their internal network. They did this by compromising a developer's account, which gave them access to parts of the company's production environment—the place where they develop and manage the software.

What Did the Hackers Take?

The intruders were able to take some of LastPass's source code (the blueprint for their software) and other technical information. However, LastPass reassured users that their master passwords—the keys to their encrypted password vaults—were not stolen. This is because of LastPass's "zero knowledge" security model, which means that even LastPass itself can't see or access your master password or the data inside your vault.

What Is LastPass Doing About It?

After discovering the breach, LastPass immediately took steps to contain the situation. They brought in a cybersecurity and forensics firm to help investigate and have since added extra security measures. As of now, they believe they've stopped any further unauthorized access and have everything under control.
